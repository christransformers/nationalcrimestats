Role descriptions

Experience in disputes

Our team's collective experience spans the full range of estate and probate disputes:

  • Contested probate — challenges to and defence of grants of probate, including allegations of forgery, undue influence, lack of capacity, and want of knowledge and approval.
  • Executor disputes — applications for removal of executors, proceedings to compel administration or distribution of an estate, and claims against executors for breach of duty.
  • Family provision claims — claims for adequate provision under Chapter 3 of the Succession Act 2006 (NSW) and Part 4 of the Succession Act 1981 (QLD), including claims by adult children, spouses, and dependants.
  • Elder financial abuse — matters involving misuse of enduring powers of attorney, NCAT/QCAT proceedings, and associated Supreme Court litigation to recover assets wrongfully taken from vulnerable older persons.
  • Urgent protective applications — freezing orders, caveats over probate, interim administration orders, and other emergency relief to preserve estate assets pending determination of a dispute.
  • Cross-jurisdictional matters — estates involving assets or parties in both NSW and Queensland, requiring coordinated proceedings in both states.
