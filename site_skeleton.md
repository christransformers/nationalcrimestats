WEBSITE SKELETON

Project working title:

\- Estate Fraud \& Probate Dispute Lawyers NSW + QLD



Positioning:

\- A specialist informational law website for people dealing with suspected estate fraud, probate fraud, executor misconduct, forged or suspicious wills, coercion of elderly people, inheritance manipulation, and elder financial abuse.

\- This is a WEBSITE, not a web app: high-trust service pages, legal guides, state-specific explainers, intake/conversion pages, FAQ hubs, and evidence/checklist resources.

\- State-law anchors: NSW pages should reference the Succession Act 2006, Queensland pages should reference the Succession Act 1981, undue-influence content should explain that probate disputes turn on coercion rather than mere persuasion, and NSW probate guidance should include the common six-month application expectation. \[web:3]\[web:12]\[web:6]\[web:11]



PRIMARY SITE GOALS

\- Convert urgent matters into phone calls and enquiry forms.

\- Educate executors, beneficiaries, attorneys, family members, and carers on what they can and cannot do.

\- Rank for NSW and QLD search intent around will disputes, probate fraud, estate fraud, elder abuse, inheritance coercion, executor misconduct, and suspicious will changes.

\- Build trust through precise legal explanations, state-specific content, practical checklists, and clear process pages.

\- Channel users into the correct pathway fast: “I’m an executor”, “I’m a beneficiary”, “I think an elderly person is being pressured”, “I need to stop assets being moved”, “I need to challenge a will”, “I need to defend a valid will”.



CORE AUDIENCES

\- Executors needing guidance, protection, or defence.

\- Beneficiaries concerned about suspicious changes, concealment, delay, or misuse of estate assets.

\- Elderly people and supporters worried about coercion, manipulation, or inheritance pressure.

\- Attorneys / people dealing with power of attorney abuse issues.

\- Adult children, siblings, stepfamily members, carers, and other interested parties in inheritance conflicts.

\- Referrers: accountants, aged-care workers, financial advisers, social workers, barristers, other solicitors.



SITE-WIDE UX RULES

\- Tone: serious, calm, high-trust, plain English, authoritative, urgent where needed.

\- Top nav must always surface: Home, Services, NSW, QLD, For Executors, For Beneficiaries, Guides, About, Contact.

\- Sticky header with call button on mobile and “Urgent estate issue?” button on desktop.

\- Persistent trust strip under header: “NSW + QLD focused”, “Probate and estate disputes”, “Elder financial abuse matters”, “Urgent advice available”.

\- Every legal explainer page uses the same reading structure:

&#x20; 2. Short plain-English summary.

&#x20; 3. “Who this helps”.

&#x20; 4. “What this issue looks like”.

&#x20; 7. “Common mistakes”.

&#x20; 8. “NSW / QLD differences”.

&#x20; 9. “How we help”.

&#x20; 10. FAQ.

&#x20; 11. CTA block.

\- Every long page includes a sticky side table of contents on desktop and a jump-menu on mobile.

\- Every page has a visible disclaimer block clarifying that site content is general information and not legal advice.

\- Every legal page includes a state toggle near the top: NSW / QLD.

\- Every page has contextual CTA choices, not just one generic contact button:

&#x20; - “Book a confidential review”.

&#x20; - “Urgent probate dispute”.

&#x20; - “Executor accused of wrongdoing”.

&#x20; - “Concern about elder coercion”.
