Client Reviews — What Our Clients Say About Working With Us

Estate disputes are deeply personal. We are grateful to the clients who have trusted us with their matters and who have taken the time to share feedback about their experience. These representative reviews reflect the service we aim to provide to every client.

Client experiences

The following quotes are representative of feedback we have received from clients. For privacy and confidentiality reasons, client names have been withheld and some details have been generalised. Each review reflects a real matter and a real outcome.

"I didn't know where to turn when I discovered my mother's will had been changed weeks before she died — while she was in hospital and heavily medicated. The team explained exactly what we needed to prove and built a case I didn't think was possible. The will was set aside and her true wishes were honoured."

— Beneficiary, NSW. Will challenge on grounds of lack of capacity and undue influence.

"As an executor I was completely overwhelmed. A beneficiary was accusing me of misconduct and I didn't know how to defend myself. The team guided me through every step, handled the correspondence, and ultimately the allegations were withdrawn. I could not have done it without them."

— Executor, QLD. Defended allegations of executor misconduct.

"My father was being pressured by a new 'friend' who had isolated him from the family. We were terrified he would change his will. The team acted quickly, helped us get protective orders through the tribunal, and the situation was stabilised before any lasting damage was done."

— Family member, NSW. Urgent elder financial abuse intervention.

"The executor had been sitting on the estate for over two years with no explanation and no distributions. I was a beneficiary and couldn't get any information. Within weeks of engaging the team, accounts were produced and distributions commenced. It was such a relief."

— Beneficiary, QLD. Compelling administration of an estate.

Common themes in client feedback

While every client's experience is unique, certain themes emerge consistently in the feedback we receive:

🎯

Clarity

Clients consistently tell us they valued clear, plain-English explanations of complex succession law concepts. Estate litigation can be confusing; we work hard to make it understandable.

⏱

Responsiveness

Clients appreciate being kept informed. We return calls, respond to emails, and provide regular updates — especially when matters are moving quickly or court deadlines are approaching.

💪

Tenacity

Estate disputes can be long and difficult. Clients value that we stay the course — that we prepare thoroughly, negotiate firmly where appropriate, and are ready to litigate when necessary.

💖

Empathy

Behind every estate dispute is a family in distress. Clients tell us they appreciate being treated with genuine care and respect — not just as a legal file, but as a person going through a hard time.

💰

Transparency

Clients value honest discussions about costs, prospects, and realistic outcomes. No one wants surprises — least of all in litigation. We set expectations clearly from the outset.

🏆

Results

Ultimately, clients come to us to resolve a problem. Whether through negotiation, mediation, or court judgment — our clients value outcomes that bring finality and allow them to move forward.

Services our clients have used

These reviews reflect experiences across a range of our practice areas. If your situation matches one of the following, we may be able to help:

Challenging a Will

Disputing the validity of a will on grounds including undue influence, lack of capacity, fraud, forgery, or suspicious circumstances.

Defending a Will

Upholding a valid will against challenge — representing executors and beneficiaries who wish to see the will honoured.

Executor Misconduct

Holding executors to account for delay, failure to account, misappropriation, self-dealing, and other breaches of duty.

Elder Financial Abuse

Protecting vulnerable older people from financial exploitation in the context of inheritance and estate planning.

Beneficiary Rights

Enforcing the rights of beneficiaries to information, proper administration, and timely distribution of estate assets.

Urgent Protection

Emergency legal steps — freezing orders, caveats, interim administration — when estate assets are at immediate risk.
